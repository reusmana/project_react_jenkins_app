# Design System Document: Organic Neo-Brutalism

## 1. Overview & Creative North Star: "The Digital Curator"
This design system is built for high-impact, editorial storytelling. We are moving away from the "template" look of the web and toward a "Digital Curator" aesthetic. This North Star balances the raw, honest structural elements of Neo-Brutalism (thick borders, bold type) with the refined, airy grace of modern minimalism (soft cream palettes, organic shapes).

The goal is **Intentional Asymmetry**. Do not center everything. Let elements overlap, break the grid with organic containers, and use extreme scale shifts in typography to create a rhythm that feels curated rather than generated.

---

## 2. Color & Surface Philosophy
The palette is rooted in a "Soft Cream" base to move away from the sterile hospital-white of standard portfolios, providing an immediate "expensive" tactile feel.

### Surface Hierarchy & Nesting
We do not use drop shadows to create depth; we use **Tonal Layering**.
- **Base Layer:** `surface` (#fafaf3) — The foundation of the page.
- **Sectioning:** Use `surface-container-low` (#f5f4ed) to define large content areas.
- **Interactive Layers:** Use `surface-container-highest` (#e3e3dc) for nested bento-grid items.

### The "No-Line" Rule (Internal)
While Neo-Brutalism traditionally uses lines, we reserve the **Charcoal (#1A1A1A)** 2px or 3px border *only* for primary structural components (cards, buttons, bento boxes). For internal sectioning, prohibit 1px solid borders. Boundaries must be defined through background color shifts or the **Ghost Border** fallback (using `outline-variant` at 15% opacity).

### Glass & Gradient Soul
To add a "Signature" feel, apply a subtle linear gradient to main CTAs transitioning from `primary` (#ae2f34) to `primary-container` (#ff6b6b). For floating navigation or overlays, use **Glassmorphism**:
- **Fill:** `surface` at 70% opacity.
- **Effect:** `backdrop-blur: 20px`.
- **Border:** 2px solid `on-background` (#1b1c18).

---

## 3. Typography: Editorial Impact
The typography strategy relies on high-contrast scales. We use **Space Grotesk** (Display/Headline) for its geometric, brutalist personality and **Manrope** (Body/Labels) for its professional clarity.

- **Display-LG (3.5rem):** Set with `-0.04em` letter spacing. Use this for hero statements that overlap image containers.
- **Headline-MD (1.75rem):** The workhorse for section titles. Always paired with a 2px `Charcoal` bottom border or an organic accent shape.
- **Body-LG (1rem):** High line-height (`1.6`) to ensure the "Natural and Bright" mood is maintained.
- **Label-SM (0.6875rem):** Uppercase with `0.1em` letter spacing. Used for categories and metadata.

---

## 4. Elevation & Depth
In this system, elevation is a physical stack, not a light source.

*   **The Layering Principle:** Depth is achieved by placing `surface-container-lowest` cards on `surface-container-low` backgrounds. This creates a "soft lift."
*   **Ambient Shadows:** If an element must float (e.g., a modal), use an ultra-diffused shadow: `0px 20px 40px rgba(27, 28, 24, 0.06)`. Never use pure black shadows.
*   **Organic Containers:** Avoid perfect circles or squares. Use `border-radius` values from the scale (e.g., `xl: 0.75rem`) but occasionally "break" one corner to `none` or `full` to mimic organic, pebble-like shapes.

---

## 5. Components

### Buttons
*   **Primary:** `primary-container` (#ff6b6b) background, 2px `Charcoal` border, `bold` Manrope text. Add a "hard shadow" (2px 2px 0px #1A1A1A) that disappears on `active` state.
*   **Secondary:** `surface` background, 2px `Charcoal` border.
*   **Tertiary:** No border, `label-md` type with an underline that expands on hover.

### Bento-Box Grid (Skills & Projects)
*   **Style:** Forbid dividers. Use `surface-container-highest` background for each cell.
*   **Border:** 2px `Charcoal` border.
*   **Spacing:** Use `spacing-6` (2rem) between cells to allow the "Cloud Blue" secondary background to breathe through the gaps.

### Input Fields
*   **Surface:** `surface-container-low`.
*   **Border:** Bottom-only 3px `Charcoal` border.
*   **State:** On focus, the background shifts to `secondary-container` (#79f3ea) with a smooth 300ms transition.

### Interactive "Pebbles" (Action Chips)
*   Small, fully rounded (`full`) shapes using `secondary-fixed` (#7cf6ec). These should "float" near images or text blocks to act as tags or tooltips.

---

## 6. Do's and Don'ts

### Do:
*   **Do** use extreme white space. If you think there’s enough room, add `spacing-8` more.
*   **Do** overlap typography. Let a `display-lg` heading sit 20px over the edge of a project image.
*   **Do** use organic shapes for image masks (e.g., a "blob" mask with a thick Charcoal stroke).

### Don't:
*   **Don't** use 1px grey lines. They look "default." If a separator is needed, use a `spacing-px` height `surface-container-highest` block.
*   **Don't** use standard "drop shadows" on cards. Rely on the 2px Charcoal border and tonal shifts.
*   **Don't** center-align long blocks of body text. Keep editorial layouts left-aligned or staggered for a bespoke feel.

---

## 7. Spacing & Rhythm
Rhythm is dictated by the `1.4rem` (`spacing-4`) base unit. 
- **Component Padding:** `spacing-4` (vertical) / `spacing-6` (horizontal).
- **Section Gaps:** `spacing-20` (7rem) or `spacing-24` (8.5rem). 
Large gaps are essential to making the "Neo-Brutalist" elements feel "High-End" rather than cluttered.
